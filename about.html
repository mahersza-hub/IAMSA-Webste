The Problem

Medicine is changing.
Students aren't prepared.

Over 75% of medical students receive no formal AI education in their curriculum. Meanwhile, 2 in 3 physicians now use AI in clinical practice.

The NHS has invested over £150 million through the NHS AI Lab. Regulatory frameworks are evolving. Clinical AI tools are in wards, radiology suites, and pathology labs — right now.

IAMSA exists to close this gap. Clinically grounded, peer-delivered, internationally connected. Not a tech club. Not a theory society.

The Lancet Digital Health (2023) found that 72% of final-year medical students felt unprepared to use AI tools in clinical practice, despite 61% already encountering them on placements.

NHS England (2024) confirmed that AI-assisted diagnosis is now active in over 50 NHS trusts, covering radiology, pathology, cardiology, and dermatology.

GMC Tomorrow's Doctors is being updated to include digital literacy competencies — making AI fluency a graduate requirement within the next curriculum cycle.

Our Model

Built differently
from the ground up.

🎯

Clinically Grounded

Every workshop, lecture, and resource is framed around real clinical scenarios — radiology AI, diagnostic algorithms, and NHS-deployed tools. Not abstract theory.

🌍

International by Design

Chapter model built for global scale from day one — UK, Ireland, USA, Australia, Canada, Germany, Singapore, and beyond. Connected, not siloed.

Research Connected

The Story

How IAMSA began.

1
Early 2026
The Gap Becomes Clear
Maher Nassor, a 2nd year medical student at the University of Sunderland, recognised that AI was rapidly becoming mainstream in healthcare — yet medical education had no structured pathway to prepare students for it. With AI adoption accelerating across the NHS and beyond, he saw a clear and urgent gap: medical students were graduating without the knowledge or skills to engage with the technology reshaping their future profession.
2
Spring 2026
IAMSA Is Founded
IAMSA is established as the International Association of Medical Students in AI — the first student-led society dedicated entirely to AI in clinical medicine. The four pillars are defined: Education, Research, Conferences, and Mentorship. The chapter model is architected for global scale.
3
June 2026
National Launch
IAMSA officially launches across the UK with regional leads confirmed at medical schools nationwide. The first national webinar series begins, covering AI in radiology, pathology, cardiology, and primary care. The IAMSA website goes live as the national hub for members, resources, and opportunities.
4
Late 2026
First National Conference
The inaugural IAMSA National Conference is hosted — CPD accredited, with abstract submissions, keynote clinicians and AI researchers, and an industry exhibition. International pilot chapters launch in Ireland, USA, and Australia. The first IAMSA needs assessment survey on AI in UK medical education is published.
5
2027 Onwards
International Scale
Full international chapter network. Annual International Medical AI Symposium. GMC curriculum consultation contribution. Position statement on AI in medical education. Industry partnership programme generating student research funding worldwide.
