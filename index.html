Why We Exist

Medicine is changing.
Students aren't prepared.

Over 75% of medical students receive no formal AI education in their curriculum. Meanwhile, 2 in 3 physicians now use AI in clinical practice — a figure that doubled in just two years.

The NHS AI Lab, launched in 2019, made an initial commitment of £250 million, with £143.5 million allocated (npj Digital Medicine, 2025). Regulatory frameworks are evolving. Clinical AI tools are in wards, radiology suites, and pathology labs — right now.

IAMSA exists to close this gap. Clinically grounded, peer-delivered, internationally connected. Not a tech club. Not a theory society. A community of future doctors preparing for the medicine they will actually practice.

Clinically Grounded

Every workshop, lecture, and resource is framed around real clinical scenarios — not abstract algorithms.

What We Do

Four pillars. One mission.

01
Teaching Series & Workshops
Nationally coordinated, specialty-specific AI education delivered peer-to-peer at every medical school.
  • Specialty AI workshops — surgery, radiology, pathology
  • Clinical AI ethics and patient safety
  • Practical AI tools for medical students
  • International guest lecturer programme
02
Research Opportunities
Connecting medical students to AI research projects, collaborations, and publication pathways.
  • National student AI research network
  • Collaborative systematic reviews in AI/medicine
  • Industry research partnership programme
  • Annual student research competition
  • Publication mentorship pathway
03
Conferences
Annual national conference and international symposium — CPD accredited, student and clinician facing.
  • Annual UK National IAMSA Conference
  • International Medical AI Symposium
  • CPD accreditation for all events
  • Abstract submission and oral presentations
  • Industry exhibition and networking
04
Mentorship
Connecting students to AI researchers, clinical informaticians, and industry professionals.
  • 1:1 mentor matching programme
  • Clinician-researcher mentor network
  • Industry career pathway guidance
  • Fellowship and grant signposting
  • International mentorship exchange
Join the Team

We are recruiting regional leads now.

We need one regional lead per UK medical school — and international representatives. This is a role that shapes the society from day one.

🇬🇧 UK Regional Lead
One per UK medical school. You are the IAMSA presence at your institution — organising local events, recruiting members, and connecting your school to the national network.
  • Medical student at a UK medical school
  • Interest in AI and clinical technology
  • Commitment of 2–4 hours per month
  • Named on all IAMSA communications
🌍 International Representative
Founding international representatives in Ireland, USA, Canada, Australia, and Europe. You establish IAMSA's presence in your country and build the local chapter network.
  • Medical student or resident doctor
  • Ability to recruit locally
  • Named representative status
  • Direct line to IAMSA national committee
⚙️ National Committee
Core national committee roles: President, Vice President, Research Lead, Events Lead, Education Lead, Communications Lead, and International Liaison.
  • UK medical student or resident doctor
  • Leadership experience preferred
  • Commitment of 4–8 hours per month
  • Named committee member on all outputs
🔬 Research Working Group
Lead IAMSA's research agenda — coordinating the national AI in medicine needs assessment, collaborative publications, and research partnerships with academic institutions.
  • Research experience or strong interest
  • Systematic review or primary research
  • Named on all IAMSA publications
  • Direct collaboration with IAMSA advisors
📢 Communications & Media
Build IAMSA's national and international presence — social media, newsletter, press releases, and media strategy. Shape how the world sees this society.
  • Medical student or resident doctor
  • Social media or communications experience
  • Creative and digitally literate
  • Flexible hours — output based
🏥 Clinical Advisory Panel
Senior clinicians, AI researchers, and clinical informaticians who advise IAMSA's educational content, endorse the society, and mentor student members.
  • Consultant, registrar, or researcher
  • Active interest in clinical AI
  • Named advisor on IAMSA platform
  • Invited to speak at IAMSA events
